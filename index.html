Why is Publishing Significant to Me?

Jessie Willcox Smith, Child Reading, Public Domain

Humble Beginnings

As a child, one of the first activities I was introduced to was reading books. As my mother always said,

Reading is like working out for your brain.
At first, my mother would read them to me before bed, then, once I learned to read for myself, I always had my nose in a book. This is where my love of reading and the written word started.

Early Writings

As I grew I tried to write my own stories, and while I liked what I wrote, I was never too excited to share it with the world. I liked writing for myself and myself alone. I knew that when I grew up I wanted to be a part of giving more stories to the world, but unfortunately, you can’t make a career out of writing for yourself.

Into the World of Publishing

Publishing is significant to me because a career in this field will help be to do two things,

  1. Be a part of the creative writing process without having to put my own work out there.
  2. Be a part of a team that helps make other people's aspirations come true.

I am already getting a start in the publishing world as one of two Co-Editors-in-Chief of the Northern Lights Literary and Arts Journal which everyone should submit to if they haven't already. Pieces can be submitted on line using the Northen Lights website.
